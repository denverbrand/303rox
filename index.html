What is HYROX?

The format, in plain English
The race
HYROX is a global fitness race held indoors. Every athlete does the exact same workout — 8 km of running broken up by 8 functional workout stations, done back-to-back, against the clock.
The loop
1 km run → 1 station → repeat × 8.
Stations: SkiErg, Sled Push, Sled Pull, Burpee Broad Jumps, Row, Farmer's Carry, Sandbag Lunges, Wall Balls. Same order, same weights, every race, everywhere.
Who it's for
Anyone with a base of fitness — runners, lifters, CrossFitters, ex-athletes, weekend warriors. Most first-timers finish between 1:30 and 2:00. Open-division averages sit around 1:25 (men) and 1:40 (women). No qualifying time required.
Why it's blowing up
Standardized format = your time in Denver is comparable to a time in Berlin, London, or Chicago. It's a real, rankable race for people who train but aren't elite endurance athletes. Globally it's the fastest-growing fitness sport on the planet.
Divisions
OPEN
Standard weights. Most first-timers race here. Men & Women.
PRO
Heavier sled, heavier sandbag, heavier wall ball. For experienced racers.
DOUBLES
Team of 2. Split work however you want. M / W / Mixed.
RELAY
Team of 4. Each person does 2 of the 8 station/run loops.
ADAPTIVE
Modified format for adaptive athletes.

The Plan
12 weeks · beginner framework
Wk 1–3
Foundation. Build aerobic base. 3–4 days per week. Easy runs (Z2), basic compound lifts (squat, deadlift, press), movement quality work.
Wk 4–6
Hybrid intro. Add running intervals plus a compromised finisher once a week. Introduce sleds, ski erg, rowing. Practice wall ball cadence.
Wk 7–9
Volume. Run plus station EMOMs. Burpee broad jump volume. Sandbag lunge endurance. Speed work into single-leg strength.
Wk 10–11
Race specificity. Two full or half race simulations. Dial in pacing, transitions, fueling. Test race-day shoes and bib position.
Wk 12
Taper. Cut volume by 40–50%. Maintain intensity. Sleep, hydration, mobility. Sharpen — don't add fitness.

Tips that move the needle

  1. HYROX is ~50% running — eight 1km runs. If you can't run sub-7:00 fresh, that's your first project.
  2. Train compromised running: 200m sprint, 20 wall balls, 200m sprint. This is the race.
  3. The sled is a grip and posture battle. Push from the hips, drive low, keep the chest tall.
  4. Wall balls win or lose races. Sets of 25 or 30 with controlled breathing beat heroic singles.
  5. Run a full simulation 3–4 weeks out, not the week before. Recovery time matters.
  6. Single-leg strength is under-trained. Bulgarian splits and step-ups matter more than you think.
  7. Train at altitude when you can. Denver sits at 5,280 ft. The run will tax you more than expected.

The Stations

1 km run → station → repeat × 8
01
SkiErg
1,000 m
Double-pole motion. Stay tall, drive from lats and core. Don't redline here — this sets the tone.
02
Sled Push
50 m
Heavy. Low hip angle, hands at shoulder height, drive through the toes. Grip matters more than strength.
03
Sled Pull
50 m
Pull backward via rope. Hand-over-hand, fall away from the sled, keep the rope tight.
04
Burpee BJ
80 m
Burpee → broad jump. Find a rhythm you could hold for 4 minutes. Don't sprint into a wall.
05
Row
1,000 m
Concept2. Long drive, controlled recovery. Target ~1:50–2:10/500m. Big legs, big finish.
06
Farmer's
200 m
Two kettlebells (24/16 kg open). Quick steps, tall posture, lockout grip. Fast walking beats stopping.
07
SB Lunges
100 m
Sandbag on the back. Knee taps the floor, full step. Quads will burn. Keep the cadence going.
08
Wall Balls
100
100 reps for all divisions. Break early into sets of 25 — saving reps for the finish loses time.
Cheat Sheet · kg ↔ lbs
Pick your division — every weighted station in kg and lbs. Doubles teams use the same weights as the equivalent solo division. Mixed Doubles uses Open M.

The Kit

Don't overthink it — except the shoes

Shoes

Your single most important piece. Prioritize grip, stability, and a feel you can run on. Sleds reward an outsole that bites carpet pile.

  • Puma Deviate Nitro Elite 4 HYROX — engineered specifically for the race
  • Puma Deviate Nitro Elite 3 — fast, light, snappy toe-off; good landings
  • Saucony Endorphin Speed / Pro — Speedroll geometry runs fast, stable enough for stations. Popular HYROX pick.
  • Nike Vaporfly / Alphafly — fast but unstable on sleds; only if you'll run great
  • Reebok Nano / Nike Metcon — lifters' picks; slower on the run
  • $50 shoes + elite fitness beats $500 shoes + beginner fitness. Fitness wins.

Race-Day Checklist

Practice this once in training. Show up on race day knowing nothing is new.

  • Race shoes plus a backup pair
  • HYROX bib and ankle chip (don't forget the chip)
  • Tight, sweat-wicking shorts and a single-layer top
  • Anti-chafe (under arms, inner thighs, nipples)
  • Soft-flask with electrolytes for warm-up
  • One caffeinated gel ~20 min pre-start — nothing experimental
  • Lifting belt — optional, allowed at most stations
  • Recovery clothes and flip-flops for after

Hydration at altitude

Denver sits at 5,280 ft. You'll lose water 30–40% faster than at sea level. Show up dehydrated and the race ends at station 4. Race week is when this matters most.

  • Race week: 80–100 oz of water daily — more than you think
  • Electrolytes: 1–2 servings/day (LMNT, Liquid IV, salt + lemon)
  • Test your race-day flask and mix in training — never debut on race day
  • Pre-race morning: 16 oz with electrolytes 2 hrs before, stop sipping 30 min out
  • Caffeine: cap at 1× your normal dose — altitude amplifies stimulants
  • The pee test: light yellow morning of race day = good; clear = overhydrated; dark = catch up
  • Day before alcohol: skip it. Dehydrating + altitude = bad combination
  • Post-race: 24 oz with electrolytes within 30 min, then sip steadily for 2 hrs

May 22
2026

Elite 15 qualification overhauled — points, not times

HYROX has scrapped time-based Elite 15 qualifying. As of July 2026, Singles count their best 5 races and Doubles their best 3 over a rolling 365-day window, ranked by points and percentile. A HYROX Athlete Licence is now mandatory, Elite Doubles partners must share a passport, and a new Division 1 tier opens up below Elite 15 (ranks ~16–30). Denver in November falls under the new system — Pro racers should plan a season, not a one-shot.
